      Mentioned in your context — this is a real differentiator for firms that read beyond technical identity (Citadel cultural fit, Two Sigma intellectual breadth, Bridgewater principles-based interviews). Use sparingly and only when it lands organically.

      When it works: A coffee chat veers into "what else are you into" or "what books are you reading." A Bridgewater-style behavioral question about how you think. A Jane Street fit chat where they're probing intellectual style.

      The hook: The Vedanta / Samkhya distinction between observer and observed, or the Yoga Sutras framing of vrittis (mental fluctuations as noise to be filtered) maps directly onto signal-vs-noise epistemology.       When NOT to use it: First-round technical screens. Anything where time pressure matters. Don't lead with it on resumes. It works as the second or third thing they learn about you, never the first.
